Hydrosphere

Around 75% of our planet is covered with water. The 5 oceans (Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Arctic, And Southern) cover 71% of the Earthâ€™s surface and contain 97.5% of the water. This means that most of our water is salty and only a small amount in drinkable fresh water.

Freshwater

Freshwater comes from precipitation. The water when reaching the ground will either sink into ground and combine with groundwater, or start flowing into rivers. Water will always flow on the path of least resistance (basically from higher elevation to lower), and this is the reason why rivers flow into the oceans (lowest points). Watersheds / drainage basins is the land that water drains off of in order to join rivers. The water drags along sediments and nutrients while flowing. This makes the coasts of rivers very fertile as well as the end. Floodplains are areas around the rivers that periodically flood and riparians are the forests that grow along river edges. Life thrives in these areas due to the abundance of water and nutrients from the rivers. Rivers deposit sediments while entering the oceans, creating fertile deltas.
